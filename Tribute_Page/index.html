SHRADHA KHAPRA

Co-founder of Apna College

Early Life

Shradha Khapra was born on 1st August, 1999 in a small village of Harayana, India. She completed her secondary education from Jain Bharati Mrigavati Vidyalaya, Delhi, and secured 10 CGPA in 10th grade and 94.4% in 12th grade. She ranked approximately 2500th in the general category in JEE Main 2017.

Education

Shradha completed her Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from Netaji Subhas Institute of Technology, Delhi (NSIT), where she became learned several valuable skills, including Full Stack Development, C++, Data Structures, Python programming and MySQL.

Carrier

After completing her degree, Shradha undertook internships at Microsoft as a Software Engineer Intern and at DRDO as a Research Trainee. She then joined Microsoft as a full-time Software Engineer and worked as an SDE. Her technical skills include C++, Python, Java, Data Structure, HTML, Web Development, and Blockchain.

Following her Passion

While working at Microsoft, Shradha received an offer from Aman Dhattarwal to work on his YouTube channel, Apna College, to teach students coding skills.

Shradha, who hails from a small village and understands the lack of education in India, accepted the offer and began teaching a placement-oriented course in C++ in 2020. Her teaching methods gained popularity and went viral, prompting her to quit her job at Microsoft to fulfill her mission of teaching coding to India.

Currently, Shradha is a full-time YouTuber, uploading videos on her channel that include interview experiences, job and placement tips, and competitive programming advice. Apna College has over 3.34 million subscribers on YouTube.

Objective

Her main objective to leave her job was to educate the Indians about coding as students of India had no quality knowledge of Coding Languages, Web Development, Placements, how to crack coding Interviews, etc. She helped millions of students to reach become job ready and grab a Job in top tech firms.

My Personal Experience

I started watching coding lectures at Apna College on YouTube. The first lecture that I watched was of Arrays. I was never able to understand coding taught in my college but Shradha Khapra made it possible. I loved the way she taught and made even the most complex topics a cake-walk. Topics like Recursion, Backtracking, OOPS, etc were some of the concepts I could never grasp, but after understanding them from Shradha, made me develop interest in these as well.

I have currently puchased two paid courses, one is of Data Structures and Algorithms using Java and another is of Full Stack Web Development, in order to learn and grow in tech field. Shradhha teaches in a way that all my basics are cleared and I find myself placement ready.
